About

Welcome! I'm a writer and grad student based in Boston. I tell stories about bodies, minds, and popular culture.

These days, I'm especially interested in vision. I have visual snow syndrome and convergence insufficiency. See the world through my eyes through my vision simulator.

If you're interested in learning more about binocular vision impairment or sharing your story vision with me, I'd love to hear from you!

Bio

Nina Wattenberg is a M.S. student in Media, Medicine, and Health at Harvard Medical School. She holds a B.A. in American Studies from Smith College. Previously, she's written for NASA's Universe of Learning podcast "Diaries of the Cosmos" and conducted audience research at the National Museum of American History. Currently, her area of interest is the emotional impact of binocular vision dysfunction.

When she's not writing, you can find her reading, doing vision therapy, and people-watching in Boston.
